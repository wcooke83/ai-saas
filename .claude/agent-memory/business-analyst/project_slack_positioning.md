---

VocUI's Slack integration is a deployment channel for custom, knowledge-grounded chatbots — not a general AI assistant. The core differentiation is BYO knowledge base (RAG on user docs/PDFs/URLs), source transparency, and multi-channel parity (same bot on Slack + website widget).

**Why this matters:** Slack AI operates on Slack's own data (messages, threads, canvases). VocUI operates on the customer's private external documentation. These are different jobs-to-be-done: Slack AI surfaces what was said internally; VocUI surfaces what was documented externally (SOPs, policies, product docs, FAQs). Not directly competing.

**Recommended positioning shift (March 2026):** Stop framing VocUI Slack as "AI for Slack" — Slack owns that label now. Reframe as "your knowledge base, deployed to Slack." Emphasize the cross-channel, single-source-of-truth story (same bot answers on the website widget AND in Slack). Target buyers who want control over what the bot knows and cites.

**Risk flag:** Current /slack-chatbot landing page hero and features don't acknowledge Slack AI at all. Prospects who already pay for Slack AI may dismiss VocUI as redundant without reading the differentiation case. A single "How is this different from Slack AI?" FAQ entry or comparison callout would close this gap cheaply.

**How to apply:** When drafting any Slack-related copy, messaging, or user stories, anchor on the knowledge-grounding angle, not the "AI in Slack" angle. The deployment channel (Slack) is secondary to the knowledge base ownership story.
